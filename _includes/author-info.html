Technical Themes

The technical content at the conference will be categorised under the following high level themes to allow conference attendees to quickly identify aspects of key interest. All papers submitted to the conference will have to include modelling or analysis methods based on probabilistic techniques in order to ensure relevance to all conference delegates and to maintain the focused scope of this conference.

Asset Health and Management
  • Reliability-centred maintenance and asset management
  • Power quality and condition monitoring
  • End-of-life prognosis and ageing assessment tools
Dealing With Extreme Events
  • Risk management and decision making under uncertainty
  • High impact low probability events
  • Cascading failures in electric power systems
  • Resilience enhancement and optimisation against extreme events
  • Outage data analysis and data-driven resilience assessment tools
Network Planning and Operation
  • Handling correlated uncertainties and big data
  • Probabilistic load and generation forecasting
  • Integration of uncertain energy sources
  • Stochastic/probabilistic optimisation and simulation techniques
  • Stochastic/probabilistic social and behavioural modelling and technological impacts
Whole Systems Analysis
  • Reliability of interdependent critical infrastructure systems
  • Probabilistic modelling of cyber-physical systems
  • Probabilistic modelling of multi-energy systems
  • Probabilistic modelling and simulation of integrated energy systems

Call for Papers

The link to submit your full paper will appear on the website in due course. Please carefully read the instructions below before submitting your paper. You can also download Call For Papers as a PDF document.

To comply with IEEE guidelines, your paper should satisfy the following requirements:

  • the paper must fit the scope of the conference (if you are not sure, please get in touch to ask).
  • the paper must not exceed the 6 page limit.
  • the layout of the paper submitted must strictly follow the IEEE PES conference paper template. Please do not submit using the “Transactions/Journal Papers” template.

Corresponding authors will be notified by email and will receive the instructions to transfer the copyright of their paper to IEEE. Note that this is required to include your paper in the Proceedings. In addition, note that papers will only be included in IEEE Xplore if presented by one of the authors.

Important dates:

  • Deadline: 15 December 2021
  • Notification of acceptance: 15 March 2022
  • Final paper uploads: 15 April 2022

Roy Billington Student Paper Award

To acknowledge and encourage excellence in power system uncertainty research by students, a prize for the “Best Student Paper” will be awarded. The Roy Billinton Award recognizes outstanding work by students in the field of Probabilistic Methods and Practical Approaches for managing Risk and Uncertainty in the Electric Power Industry. Eligibility for the award is restricted to papers which are first-authored by a student. More information regarding the award will be announced at a later time. If your paper is eligible for this award and if you want to be considered for the Roy Billinton Award then please select the appropriate box in the submission system.

Call for Tutorials

We invite proposals for half or full day tutorials. Tutorials will take place on Sunday 12th June 2022 from 9:00am to 5:00pm. Half day tutorials will be comprised of two 1.5 hour blocks during the morning or afternoon. Full day tutorials will be comprised of four 1.5 hour blocks. The content of the tutorials can be either broad to serve as an introduction to a topic, or deep in order to focus on cutting-edge developments. You can also download Call For Tutorials as a PDF document.

  • Tutorial title
  • Tutorial organiser (name, affiliation, contact e-mail, and a short CV or link)
  • List of speakers (name, affiliation, contact e-mail, and a short CV or link). It can be only the organiser.
  • Abstract. No more than 300 words.
  • Length. Half day (two 1.5 hour blocks) or full day (four 1.5 hour blocks).
  • Structure. Topics to be addressed in each block.
  • Special requirements. Please indicate whether there is any special requirements for the attendees (e.g., attendees need to bring laptops or have specialised software already installed).
  • Previous experience. Please state if you have already organized a tutorial on the same or similar topic in a previous conference. Provide details as necessary (e.g., title, year, number of attendees).

Tutorials will be allocated infrastructure (room, AV equipment, etc.) and will be advertised along with the different activities of PMAPS 2022. The organiser (or designated speaker) will be given one (1) free registration for the whole conference. However, it is a requirement that all other speakers register for the conference unless their attendance is limited to the corresponding tutorial. The PMAPS 2022 conference organisers will not cover travel-related expenses. All income resulting from tutorial ticket sales goes to PMAPS 2022.

Important dates:

  • Deadline: 15 December 2021
  • Notification of acceptance: 14 January 2022
  • Final tutorial presentation uploads (PPT or PDF): 27 May 2022

Paper Preparation

To comply with IEEE guidelines, your paper should satisfy the following requirements:

  • the paper must fit the scope of the conference (if you are not sure, please get in touch to ask).
  • the paper must not exceed the 6-page limit.
  • the abstract must not exceed 150 words ( Formatted Conference Paper for the IEEE PES).
  • the keywords must not exceed 5 words ( Formatted Conference Paper for the IEEE PES).
  • the layout of the paper submitted must strictly follow the IEEE A4 format LaTeX or Microsoft Word templates. Make sure it is A4 and not Letter format.

Following acceptance, corresponding authors will be notified by email and will receive the instructions to transfer the copyright of their paper to IEEE. Note that this is required to include your paper in the Proceedings. In addition, note that papers will only be included in IEEE Xplore if presented by one of the authors at the conference.

Review Process

To ensure high-quality contributions and an excellent conference, all papers will go through a single-blind review and selection process. Following review by expert peers, led by the International Technical Advisory Committee, each paper will be classified as “not-accepted”, “accepted”, or “conditionally accepted”.

The authors of “conditionally accepted” papers will be invited to submit a revised version after addressing the reviewers’ comments. “Conditionally accepted” papers that do not appropriately address reviewers’ comments will be rejected.
